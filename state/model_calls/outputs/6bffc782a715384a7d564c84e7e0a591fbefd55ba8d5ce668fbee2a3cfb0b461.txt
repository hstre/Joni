["Dense supervision methods are typically evaluated via expensive downstream training that conflates signal quality with training engineering and renders different methodological families incomparable, leading to rare benchmarking on common ground.", "We introduce QVal, a training-free testbed that directly evaluates dense supervision signals by measuring Q-alignment against a strong reference-policy, allowing comparison before training and separating signal quality from engineering choices.", "We instantiate QVal as QVal-v1.0, benchmarking 21 methods across four environments, seven families, and six model backbones with over 1.2K experiments.", "We find that simple prompting baselines consistently outperform recent dense supervision methods, and performance clusters strongly by family, with findings holding across model sizes, environments, and observation modalities."]
